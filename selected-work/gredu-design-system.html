2020

Gredu Design System

Muyas’s Contribution Recap

During that period, Muyas (was a Jr. UX-UI Designer) collaborated with W (Sr. UX-UI Designer) to initiate and conduct each process, including:

  • In-depth analysis to understand the pain points and get the actionable solution.
  • Take comprehensive audit to understand what went well and what can be improved in order to get standardized convention, workflow, and components.
  • In his early career, Muyas can form a tangible illustration of how design system can benefit the business as he learned from W.
  • Plan a fundamental basis to aligned on naming convention, tools and version control scheme, usage of reusable component, comprehensive design guideline, and all aspects of the brand.
  • Conduct a pilot test to validate the implementation and take some lesson for next iteration.

About

Existing Condition

Designers and engineers adopt individualized approaches while working on their respective products.
Some work slowly, some work fast. Those who are new usually take a little longer. And every time there is a delegation, the delegated party takes a long time to learn how the delegator works.

Product Detail

Gredu is a platform that enhances the school experience by promoting involvement and collaboration among teachers, students, and parents. Here are some of the services provided by Gredu:

  • Student App and Web: for students to access the syllabus, their grades, attendance, and learning activity schedules.
  • Teacher App and Web: for teachers to monitor student attendance and document syllabi and exams.
  • Parent App and Web: for parents to follow their children's learning progress and activities at school.
  • School Management Web: an administrative application for accessing, processing, and presenting school administration data.
  • PPDB Web: a service system for registering new students online.

Goal

Projected Business Outcome

Time and cost optimization.
*For this initiative project, we tried to aim for business outcome as an alternative from a cascading company OKR.

Objective

All teams can rapidly build consistent UIs.

Key Result

  • 100% of reusable components can be found in the Design System library and guidlines.
  • 75% of the teams are using the UI Kit in the new projects.

Process

1. Analyze The Workflow

Muyas and W initially conduct an in-depth analysis of each current product and the corresponding teams' workflow using Lightning Decision Jam (LDJ) approach. And here is the result:

2. Get The Buy-In

In the first attempt, Muyas and W were rejected by some accountable persons a couple of times because they couldn't explain the business outcome. In the second attempt, Muyas can form a tangible illustration of how design system can benefit the business as he learned from how W pitch and got support from the board directors.

3. Comprehensive Audit

Muyas and W started to dividing task to audit:

  • Naming that applied on folders, files, assets, artboards, layers, and components.
  • Tools and version control we have used.
  • All of components we have across all products.

By doing this, we understand what went well and what can be improved in order to get standardized convention, workflow, and components.

4. Plan a Fundamental Basis

  • Naming Convention
    Muyas and W proposed a naming convention for folders, files, assets, artboards, layers, and components. Here is an example of how we organized our components in Sketch App:
  • Tools and Version Control Scheme for Designers
    After further discussion with designers and engineers, we agreed to use the following tools to ensure alignment during production. We utilized Abstract for version control and tracking changes. Collaborators were allowed to create branches based on their tasks, with only the Lead Designer authorized to merge the work into the master branch after the design review.
  • Establishing Reusable Components for Different Platforms
    With a focus on modularity and scalability, Muyas and W explored various concepts and settled on a unified style for Web, Android, and iOS. This approach allowed us to create reusable components tailored to specific platforms.
  • Developing a comprehensive design guideline
    Muyas and W proposed a comprehensive design guideline that build based on in-depth interview with several designers and engineers to set a design guideline from the ground until handoff it to engineers.
  • Determining all aspects of the brand, including logo variations, product color categories, and more
    For a logo variation, Muyas and W collaborate with graphic designers as they are responsible on branding.
    For copywriting, Muyas and W collaborate with content writer (no ux writer at that time).

5. Pilot Test

  • Pilot Testing on Multiple Apps
    Muyas and W conducted a pilot project by testing the implemented strategies on the multiple apps we have.
  • Revised Version

    Takeaways

    • The Power of Standardized Conventions and Workflow: Implementing standardized conventions and workflows enhances team efficiency. No more waste of time to think and consider naming conventions themselves.
    • Efficient workflow is benefiting for the company, not only the employees: Standardized convention, workflow, and reusable components often assumed have no contribution to the business. But, as it implemented, everyone can feel we can work faster. So it means cut the timeline and cost.
    • A Path to Lasting Support: Demonstrating the benefits to stakeholders through tangible results is crucial for ensuring their engagement and support.
